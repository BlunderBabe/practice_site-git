BlunderBabble

She lives life for the stories : Thirty-one years ago, in tiny town on the border of Canada, in upstate New York, a super-hero was born...

Its 9pm Thursday night when the brilliant idea to head out of Bangkok for the weekend strikes. My pasty flesh craves a smog-free sun kiss. The din of the city resonates through my veins, into my brain; its waves wash up images of sand and sea. Its respite I need; and I need it Now.

Without hesitation, I rummage about the ruins of clothing and ‘stuff’ I call my apartment. My rucksack is packed in five minutes—leaving me with plenty of time to catch the 10:50pm train to Champorn, a port town with ferry service to Koh Tao—a southern Thailand Island Paradise, or so I’ve heard.

I’m out the door as soon as I send the week’s final work related email.

A taxi is ready and waiting outside my apartment building on Suan Plu. My ATM card, stolen a week earlier arrived today. I have credit on my phone and plenty of cash in my wallet.

There is only one person in the ticket booth queue. Everything goes smoothly, which when you’re me, should arouse suspicions immediately. But right now, I am certain that nothing can bring me down. “One way second class ticket to Champorn,” I say. “Sold out,” with two words the cashier has successfully quashed my high. I’m only relieved that I didn’t put any wagers on ‘certainty.’ For I while, I wallow in denial. I scour the passenger crowd, hoping to find someone willing to sell me their ticket…for double the price. At rock-bottom desperation—I’m ready to offer 2000 baht, and my backpack—my senses bounce back. I finally admit defeat, and leave the train station.
